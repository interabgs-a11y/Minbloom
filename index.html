Stress & Anxiety Relief
You don't have to carry it all
Simple, science-backed techniques used by counsellors worldwide.

The 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique

Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. Brings you back to the present moment instantly.

Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)

Inhale 4 counts → Hold 4 → Exhale 4 → Hold 4. Repeat 4 times. Calms the nervous system within minutes.

Write it out — brain dump

Set a timer for 5 minutes. Write every anxious thought without stopping. Don't judge, don't edit. Getting it out of your head onto paper gives you perspective.

The "is this solvable?" question

Ask yourself — is this worry something I can act on right now? If yes, make one small plan. If no, consciously choose to let it rest.

Move your body for 10 minutes

A 10-minute walk changes your brain chemistry. Movement releases endorphins and interrupts the anxiety thought loop.

Name the emotion to tame it

Don't say "I am anxious." Say "I notice I'm feeling anxious." Small shift, big difference.

Progressive muscle relaxation

Tense each muscle group for 5 seconds, then release. Start from your toes upward.

Cold water on your wrists

Running cold water on your pulse points activates the vagus nerve, quickly lowering your heart rate.

Prioritise one thing

Write all tasks down, pick just one that matters most today. Let the rest exist on paper, not in your head.

Challenge the "what if" spiral

Counter with: what is the evidence this will happen? What's the realistic outcome? What would I say to a friend thinking this?

Schedule your worry time

Give yourself 15 minutes at a fixed time each day to worry deliberately. Outside that window, postpone anxious thoughts.

Behavioural activation — do one small thing

Do one small enjoyable thing even when you don't feel like it. Motivation follows action, not the other way around.

Sunlight in the morning

10 minutes of morning sunlight resets your circadian rhythm and boosts serotonin.

Connection over isolation

Send one message to someone you trust. You don't have to explain everything. Just reach out.
